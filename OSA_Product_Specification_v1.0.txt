This document is the authoritative product specification for OSA. It defines what is being built, why, who it serves, how each feature works, the data model, the technical architecture, and the business model. The developer should not need any other document to begin implementation.

1.  Product Overview

1.1  What Is OSA?
OSA (Old Students Platform) is a multi-school alumni management web application. It gives old students associations — regardless of what their specific association is named — a shared digital home built around four core activities: publishing a monthly newsletter, raising funds for emergencies and school development, hosting and RSVPing to virtual events and meetups, and maintaining a private-but-searchable directory of members.

OSA is not a social network. There is no chat, no feed, no likes, and no public-facing profiles. It is a structured, purposeful platform for the specific workflows of an alumni community: communicate, contribute, convene, and connect — on the members' own terms.

1.2  Why OSA Exists
Ghanaian secondary school alumni associations — and many across anglophone West Africa — are some of the most tightly-bonded communities in the world. AMOSA at Aggrey Memorial, AOSA at Achimota, PREOBA at Prempeh, WEGOSA at Wesley Girls — these groups produce doctors, lawyers, diplomats, and engineers who remain fiercely loyal to their schools and year groups for life. Yet they have no purpose-built digital infrastructure. WhatsApp groups fragment, email threads get lost, fundraisers run through personal accounts, and institutional memory disappears.

OSA solves this. It gives every school, every year group, and every individual member a structured, dignified, and permanent digital space — while generating a recurring revenue stream for ICUNI Labs.

1.3  Business Context — ICUNI Labs
OSA is a product of ICUNI Labs, the digital products division of ICUNI Group based in Accra, Ghana. ICUNI Labs builds, hosts, and maintains the platform. Schools and their alumni associations license access to the platform as subscribers. ICUNI Labs holds Super Admin credentials across all schools and provides technical support, updates, and custom development.

The platform also serves as a direct marketing channel for ICUNI Group. Every old student who joins OSA is exposed to ICUNI Labs branding and, by extension, to ICUNI Starterclass, ICUNI Studios, ICUNI Consult, and ICUNI Connect. The product owner (Menelek Makonnen) is personally present on the platform as a member of the Class of 2012 at Aggrey Memorial — the first school on the platform.

1.4  Core Product Principles
No chat. Communication happens through the newsletter and event systems only. This is by design — it keeps the platform purposeful and signal-to-noise high.
Privacy by default. Every user controls exactly who sees each piece of their contact information. Privacy decisions are enforced server-side.
School isolation. Each school's data is completely separate. A member of one school cannot access another school's members, posts, or campaigns unless they are a platform-level executive or IT staff.
Year group as identity. Every user's primary identity on the platform is their year group, house, final class, and cheque colour. These are fixed at registration and cannot be changed by the user.
Governed, not ungoverned. Every school and every year group has a defined executive structure. The newsletter is editor-reviewed. Campaigns and events are admin-created. The platform has clear authority at every level.
Built to scale. The architecture supports an unlimited number of schools and year groups. Adding a new school requires one spreadsheet, one Apps Script deployment, and one config row.

2.  Users, Roles &amp; Permissions

2.1  User Types
Every person on the platform is a registered user. All users belong to exactly one school and one year group. The platform has three tiers of authority:

Member — a regular old student. Can submit posts, RSVP to events, donate, manage their own profile, and view the directory according to privacy settings.
Admin — a Year Group executive or Platform executive. Has additional capabilities within their scope (their year group or the whole platform).
Super Admin — ICUNI Labs IT department only. Has unrestricted access to all schools, all data, all settings, and all users.

4.  Feature: Newsletter

The newsletter is the platform's primary communication channel. Each year group has its own monthly newsletter. Members submit posts (short personal updates, announcements, professional news, tributes) and a Year Group executive reviews and approves each post before it is included in the month's dispatch. There is no chat. There is no comment thread. Posts that are approved become part of the permanent record.

4.3  Newsletter Dispatch
The monthly newsletter dispatch is triggered manually by the YG President or any YG executive with dispatch permission. The system collects all approved posts for the year group for the current newsletter month and sends them as a single HTML email to every member of that year group.

Email format: HTML, with the school's brand colour and year group's cheque colour as visual identifiers. Includes the school name, association name, year group year and nickname, list of posts with author names and categories, and a footer linking to OSA.
Sender: The email is sent from a configured platform address (e.g. newsletter@osa.icuni.org) via Gmail / Apps Script MailApp.
Recipients: All members of the year group whose accounts are active, regardless of their individual privacy settings — the newsletter is an internal communication.
A dispatch record is written to the newsletters sheet, including the post IDs included, recipient count, and timestamp.
Once dispatched, a newsletter month is closed. Posts cannot be retroactively added to a dispatched month.

Fundraising on OSA is designed for two distinct purposes: emergency relief and school development support. Campaigns are created by admins (not members) and are visible either to the whole school or to a specific year group. There is no self-service fundraiser — this prevents noise and keeps campaigns meaningful.

5.3  Donation Flow
Donor selects a campaign and clicks "Donate Now".
A donation modal appears showing the current total, a set of quick-select amounts (GHS 50, 100, 200, 500, 1000), and a custom amount field.
Donor confirms. The donation is recorded in the donations sheet. The campaign's raised_amount and donor_count are updated.
A receipt is sent to the donor's registered email address via Apps Script.
Note: v1 does not integrate a payment gateway. Donations are recorded as pledges / acknowledgements. Actual payment collection is managed by the YG executives via bank transfer, mobile money, or cheque. A payment_method field captures the method agreed upon. The platform records intent and tracks amounts. The Finance Executive logs, updates and manages payment for spreadsheet.

v2 Scope: Mobile money (MTN MoMo, Vodafone Cash) and card payment gateway integration to be implemented in a future version, enabling real-time fund collection and automated receipts.

5.4  Fundraising Screen — UI
Scope filter: My School | All Schools.
Type filter: All | Emergency | School Support.
Each campaign card shows: title, type indicator, school pill, description (truncated), progress bar with percentage, raised amount vs target, donor count, deadline, and latest update.
Emergency campaigns have a red left border and "Warning" indicator to communicate urgency.
Admins see a "Create Campaign" button. Members do not.

6.4  RSVP Logic
Any member can RSVP to any event visible to them.
RSVP can be cancelled before the event date.
For virtual events: the meeting link is shown only after RSVP, and only in the event detail modal. It is never shown in the card list view.
When max_attendees is reached, the RSVP button shows "Full" and is disabled.
The organiser receives no RSVP notifications in v1. RSVP counts are visible to all.

11.  Authentication &amp; Registration

11.1  Registration Flow
User visits osa.icuni.org and is presented with the multi-school landing page.
User selects their school from the school grid.
User is taken to the school's registration form.
User fills in: Full Name, Email, Password, Year Group (dropdown of available year groups), Final Class, House Name.
User submits the form. The system checks that the email is not already registered for that school.
Account is created with role = "member". A confirmation email is sent.
YG executive is not required to approve registration in v1. Registration is open and self-service.

v2 Consideration: Optional admin-approval of new registrations for schools that want tighter control over who joins.

11.2  Login Flow
User selects their school on the landing page.
User enters email and password.
Apps Script verifies the password hash against the members sheet.
On success: a session token (UUID) is generated, written to the members sheet with a 7-day expiry, and returned to the frontend.
The frontend stores the token in memory (React state) for the session. No localStorage.
Every subsequent API call includes the token. The server validates it before executing any operation.

11.3  Password Reset
User clicks "Forgot Password" on the login screen.
Enters their registered email address.
Apps Script generates a time-limited reset token (valid for 1 hour), writes it to the members sheet, and sends a reset link to the email via Gmail.
User clicks the link, enters a new password. The script verifies the reset token, updates the password hash, and clears the token.

11.4  Session Management
Sessions expire after 7 days of inactivity.
Explicit logout clears the session token from the members sheet.
The frontend checks token expiry on app load. If expired, the user is redirected to login.
IT staff can forcibly clear any user's session token (immediate logout) from the Super Admin panel.

15.2  Data Architecture
Master Spreadsheet (ICUNI Labs): Contains the schools registry, platform governance roles, IT department roster, and cross-platform analytics snapshots.
School Spreadsheet (one per school): Contains 8 sheets — year_groups, members, posts, campaigns, donations, events, rsvps, newsletters. Fully isolated from other schools.
All Sheets use UUIDs as primary keys. Foreign keys reference within the same spreadsheet for school data, or across to the Master for governance.
Privacy-related fields are columns on the members sheet (priv_email, priv_phone, etc.) — not a separate sheet. This keeps privacy enforcement fast and co-located with the member record.

15.3  API Architecture
All API calls are HTTPS POST or GET requests to the school's Apps Script Web App URL.
Every request includes a session token. The server validates the token before any operation.
POST requests send a JSON body with { action, data, token }.
GET requests send query parameters: ?action=xxx&amp;token=xxx&amp;param=xxx.
All responses are JSON: { success: true/false, data: {...}, error: "message" }.
Privacy filtering is applied server-side on every member data response.
Role checks are performed server-side on every admin action.

15.4  Frontend API Client
A central api(action, data, method) utility function handles all calls.
The session token is held in React state and injected by the utility. Never in localStorage.
On 401 (expired/invalid token): the user is logged out and redirected to login.
Optimistic UI updates are applied immediately on user actions, rolled back on API failure.
Loading states are shown during all API calls. No silent failures.

16.  Multi-School Model

OSA is designed from the ground up to support any number of schools. A school is not a feature — it is an independent data partition. Adding a school means creating a spreadsheet, deploying a script, and adding one row to the Master. Nothing else changes.

16.1  What Changes Per School
Brand colour — used throughout the UI wherever school identity is displayed.
School name and short code — displayed in all headers, school pills, and footers.
Association name — the formal name of the school's alumni body (e.g. AMOSA, AOSA, PREOBA).
Year groups — unique to each school, with their own houses, final class designations, and cheque colours.
All data — completely isolated. No school can see another school's members, posts, campaigns, or events unless explicitly set to platform scope.

16.2  What Is Shared Across Schools
Platform governance roles (President, VP, Gen. Secretary, Organiser) — these roles span all schools. Their holders are regular members of a specific school but hold cross-platform authority.
Platform-scope events — created by Platform Exec or IT and visible to old students of all schools.
The OSA brand and platform infrastructure — hosted, maintained, and owned by ICUNI Labs.
The ICUNI Labs IT department — Super Admin across all schools.

16.3  School Landing Page
The public landing page at osa.icuni.org shows all registered schools in a grid. Each school card displays the school name, short code, city, association name, founding year, and year group count. Clicking a school card takes the user to that school's login/registration page. The URL pattern is osa.icuni.org/[school_id] — e.g. osa.icuni.org/amosa.

In the short term, while OSA begins with Aggrey Memorial only, the URL osa.icuni.org may redirect directly to the Aggrey Memorial login. The multi-school landing becomes the default once a second school is onboarded.

19.  Developer Notes &amp; Hard Requirements

19.1  Non-Negotiables
Privacy enforcement is always server-side. Never trust the frontend to hide sensitive fields. The Apps Script handler filters data before returning it.
Role checks are always server-side. A user cannot escalate their own privileges by modifying frontend state.
Session tokens are never stored in localStorage or sessionStorage. React state or equivalent in-memory store only.
No chat and no comments. Do not implement these even if technically trivial.
The cheque colour system is a first-class design element, not an afterthought. It must be rendered consistently across every component that references a year group.
School data isolation is absolute. An API call for school A must never return data from school B.
The ICUNI Labs "Powered by" attribution is permanent and non-removable by any user, including Super Admin.

19.2  Code Conventions
All primary keys are UUIDs generated server-side (Apps Script Utilities.getUuid()).
All timestamps are stored in ISO 8601 format: YYYY-MM-DDTHH:mm:ss.sssZ.
All monetary amounts are stored as plain decimal numbers. Currency is always stored alongside amount.
JSON fields in Sheets (updates, post_ids) are stored as stringified JSON. Always parse/stringify safely with try/catch.
Sheets column order must exactly match the schema defined in the Backend Architecture document. Never reorder columns once deployed — use column name lookups, not column index lookups.

19.3  Testing Checklist
Privacy: create two users in different year groups. Verify that yeargroup-only fields of user A are not visible to user B.
Role enforcement: verify that a member (non-exec) receives 403 when attempting to call reviewPost or dispatchNewsletter.
Session expiry: set a short token expiry, wait, verify the user is redirected to login on the next API call.
Multi-school isolation: create two schools. Verify that a logged-in user of school A cannot retrieve members from school B.
Newsletter dispatch: approve two posts, dispatch the newsletter, verify both the dispatch record is written and the posts' newsletter_month is updated.
Campaign total: make two donations to the same campaign, verify the raised_amount and donor_count reflect both.
