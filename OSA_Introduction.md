# Introducing OSA: The Old Students Platform

**Version 1.0 | By ICUNI Labs**

## What is OSA?
OSA (Old Students Platform) is a purpose-built, multi-school alumni management web application. It provides old students associations with a shared, dignified, and permanent digital home. 

Unlike conventional social networks, OSA is structurally designed around four core activities:
1. **Communicate**: Publishing curated, editor-reviewed monthly newsletters.
2. **Contribute**: Raising funds for emergencies and school development.
3. **Convene**: Hosting and RSVPing to virtual events, physical meetups, and hangouts.
4. **Connect**: Maintaining a private, searchable directory of verified members.

There is no endless feed. There is no chat. There are no public-facing profiles. OSA is a structured space designed for high-signal communication and meaningful alumni engagement.

## Why OSA Exists
Secondary school alumni associations in West Africa (such as AMOSA, AOSA, PREOBA, WEGOSA) are some of the most tightly-bonded communities in the world. They produce leaders across all industries who remain fiercely loyal to their schools. 

Yet, their digital infrastructure is lacking:
- WhatsApp groups fracture and become noisy.
- Email threads get buried.
- Fundraisers are run informally through personal accounts.
- Institutional memory is easily lost.

OSA solves this fragmentation. It gives every school, year group, and individual member a permanent digital space, complete with governance tools for executives and privacy controls for members.

## Core Product Principles

### 1. Governed, Not Ungoverned
Every school and year group on OSA operates with defined executive structures. Content is curated; the newsletter is reviewed by editors, and campaigns and events are managed by admins. This ensures quality and reliability.

### 2. Purpose-Driven Communication
By eliminating instant messaging and comment threads, OSA keeps the signal-to-noise ratio exceptionally high. Communication happens formally through monthly newsletters and organized events.

### 3. Absolute Privacy
Users control exactly who sees every piece of their contact information—whether it is restricted to just their Year Group, open to All Old Students, or entirely Hidden.

### 4. Year Group as Identity
A member's core identity—their School, Year Group, Final Class, House, and unique "Cheque Colour"—is fixed at registration. This "DNA" forms the foundation of their interaction on the platform.

## Key Features

- **The Monthly Newsletter**: A curated dispatch of personal news, professional updates, tributes, and announcements. Members submit drafts, executives review them, and a polished HTML email is dispatched monthly.
- **Fundraising Campaigns**: Secure tracking for "Emergency" relief efforts or long-term "School Support" projects, complete with progress bars and donor transparent updates.
- **Events Management**: Seamless organization and RSVP tracking for virtual webinars, in-person meetups, and casual hangouts.
- **Dynamic Members Directory**: A searchable registry of alumni, heavily filtered by the member's personal privacy parameters.
